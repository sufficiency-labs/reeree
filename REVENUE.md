# Revenue Model

## Current Status

reeree is a personal tool being built for own use. Revenue is not a goal for Phase 1-6.

## Potential Future Revenue Streams

### Stream 1: Open Source + Sponsorship
**Source:** GitHub Sponsors, Open Collective
**Pricing:** Voluntary donations
**Target:** Developers who use reeree daily and want to support development
**Expected volume:** $0-500/mo at best. Not a business model — supplemental.

### Stream 2: Premium Features (if ever)
**Source:** Paid add-ons or hosted service
**Pricing:** TBD
**Note:** This is speculative. The core tool must remain free and open source per values. Any premium layer would be convenience (hosted daemon pools, team collaboration), never core functionality gated behind payment.

## Market Size

The market for terminal-native LLM coding tools is real and growing (Aider: 4.1M installs, OpenCode: 95K stars, Goose: 27K stars). But reeree's positioning (dispatch console, not chatbot) is novel — no direct comp for market sizing.

### Stream 3: BDFL Authority (Most Likely Path)
**Source:** Speaking, writing, consulting as the recognized creator
**Model:** Like Guido van Rossum (Python) or Linus Torvalds (Linux) — build something useful, become the recognized authority on the dispatch console paradigm, get paid for expertise. Stick out your hand and say "I need some money" for some writing or speaking and people give it to you.
**Pricing:** Market rate for expert consulting/speaking
**Note:** This requires reeree to actually succeed and the dispatch paradigm to prove itself. Not something you engineer — it's a consequence of building something genuinely good and becoming the person people associate with it.

## Path to First Dollar

Not a priority. Build for own use. Open source when ready. The tool is easy to build and fork — the value isn't in the code, it's in the paradigm and the taste that shaped it. Revenue follows from being the person who established the dispatch console as a category, not from gating the software.

Revenue is a trailing indicator of utility, not a goal. See [Profit](PROFIT.md) for sustainability model and [Values](VALUES.md#6-sufficiency-over-maximalism) for why this is enough.
